How the risk score is computed

The score is a transparent, weighted sum of the threat signals returned for the IP — the same weighted-signal method behind my other projects (PULSE-360, OPTICHAIN). Each flag that is present adds its weight; the total is capped at 100.

Bands: 0 = Clean · 1–24 = Low · 25–54 = Elevated · 55+ = High risk.

How the patch-gap check works

Your version is tested against the affected-version ranges of every published CVE for the selected product. Data comes from the NVD 2.0 API and is cross-referenced with the CISA Known Exploited Vulnerabilities catalog to flag CVEs under active attack. A CVE counts as affecting you when your version falls inside any of its vulnerable ranges (e.g. ≥ 7.0.0 and < 7.0.12). The recommended version clears every matched range — the same transparent, weighted approach behind PULSE-360 and OPTICHAIN.
